X-ray (2D radiography)
• X-rays (ionizing radiation) are generated in an X-ray tube.
• Rays pass through the body.
• Different tissues absorb radiation differently (bone absorbs
  more than soft tissue).
• A detector captures the remaining radiation.
• Produces a 2D projection image
CT (Computed
Tomography)
• Rotating X-ray source takes multiple projections around the
  body.
• Detectors measure attenuation from multiple angles.
• Computer reconstructs cross-sectional slices.
MRI (Magnetic Resonance
Imaging) – fMRI
• Strong magnetic field aligns hydrogen protons.
• Radiofrequency (RF) pulses disturb alignment.
• Protons emit signals as they relax.
• Signals are spatially encoded and reconstructed.
fMRI (Functional MRI)
• Measures BOLD signal (Blood Oxygen Level Dependent).
• Detects changes in oxygenated vs deoxygenated blood.
• Used to map brain activity.
Ultrasound
•   High-frequency sound waves emitted from a probe.
•   Waves reflect at tissue boundaries.
•   Reflected echoes are captured.
•   Time-of-flight converted into image.
Digital Pathology
• Tissue biopsy is processed and stained.
• Slide scanned using a high-resolution whole slide scanner.
• Produces gigapixel digital image (Whole Slide Image -
  WSI).

Transfer learning
Instead of training from scratch:
• Take a model pre-trained on a large
   dataset (e.g., ImageNet)
• Reuse its learned feature representations
• Fine-tune it on medical images
Common pretrained models:ResNet, VGG,
DenseNet, EfficientNet
Transfer learning
Feature Extraction
• Freeze backbone
• Train only classifier head
• Good for very small datasets
Fine-Tuning
• Unfreeze last few blocks
• Adapt to domain shift
• Most common approach
Domain-Specific Pretraining
• Instead of ImageNet:Pretrain on large medical dataset
• Then fine-tune on specific hospital dataset

R-CNNs
Selective Search :
• Look at the image and divide it into small
   patches (like puzzle pieces).
• Group together similar-looking patches
   (e.g., same color, texture, etc.).
• Combine those groups into larger
   rectangles that might contain objects.
• Output ~2000 of those rectangles (region
   proposals).

Attention U-Net model
Attention Gates (AGs) in the skip connections instead of
directly concatenating encoder features
The attention gate:
• Receives: Encoder feature map (low-level detail),
    Decoder feature map (context signal)
• Computes attention coefficients (values between 0 and 1)
• Suppresses irrelevant regions
• Enhances important regions
